Phishing simulation – you clicked the link

From: Security Awareness Training <training@security-awareness.com>

This was a simulated phishing exercise designed to show what can happen when you click a suspicious link.

In a real attack, the link could have given an attacker remote access to your machine, allowed them to steal data, or install malware.

Next time, pay attention to:

  • Unexpected or urgent security alerts
  • Generic greetings like “Dear user” instead of your name
  • Sender addresses that don’t match the real organisation
  • Links asking you to log in or enter sensitive information

You will now receive another email with a similar link from a different sender. This is your chance to spot the signs and avoid clicking.
